The Founder

Michael Esquivel

Founder, Notropia Decision Intelligence
MSHR · OD & HR Executive
25+ Years in Practice

I built Notropia for people like me.

The moment that made this real did not happen in a boardroom. It happened walking out of my therapist's office after an intense session where she caught something in me I could not see on my own.

I had been operating from a version of strength I learned early in life. My dad taught me to be strong — to push through, stay composed, and not let emotion get in the way. And that served me. It helped me perform, lead, and carry responsibility.

But in that session, something shifted. A pattern I believed was strength — something I trusted — was quietly working against me. She named it. And in that moment, the gap between how I saw myself and what was actually driving me became visible.

"That moment didn't just change me. It clarified 25 years of work."

Because I had seen that same gap in hundreds of leaders across hundreds of organizations. Leaders who looked capable, disciplined, and in control — but were unknowingly being shaped by patterns they didn't realize they were carrying. Not because they lacked skill, but because they had never been shown how to see their own thinking clearly.

The leaders who navigated complexity differently didn't have better techniques. They had a different relationship with how they think. Their identity wasn't separate from how they led. It was the foundation of it.

"Who you are is how you lead."

That truth has always been there. But what hasn't existed is a practical way to work at that level — inside the thinking itself, where decisions actually begin.

I built Notropia for people like me. Leaders who carry the pressure of high-stakes decisions every day — and have never had a thinking partner in the room to help them shoulder the pressure, and that meets them where they are.

From Descartes
to Krisis

Descartes said it first — I think therefore I am. Identity originates in the act of thinking. It is not downstream of your decisions. It is the condition that produces them.

The ancient Greeks built entire philosophical systems around this. Socrates. Aristotle. The Stoics. They weren't writing theory — they were building operating systems for human judgment under pressure. Krisis — κρίσις — was their word for the decisive moment. Not crisis in the modern sense of panic. The moment where clarity and choice converge. The moment of judgment.

That is exactly what Emma and Sarah are built to hold.

Notropia — νοοτροπία

Notropia comes from the Greek nootropia — mind-turning. The act of turning toward your own thinking. Examining what is driving the decision before the decision is made.

The name wasn't engineered. It was already there — already true to how the work had always been described. Notropia Decision Intelligence is what happens when a 25-year OD practitioner stops watching leaders make the same structural decisions with the same invisible blind spots, and decides to build the system that should have existed all along.

Not designed from theory. Built from instinct, pressure-tested in real sessions, and refined through the same pattern intelligence it delivers to the leaders who use it.

Lead with intention instead of inheritance.

Notropia will always meet you where you are.
